INDIAN
FESTIVALS
Indian festivals like Diwali, Holi, and Eid celebrate unity, culture, and traditions, filled with joy, colors, and rituals.
DIWALI
Festival of Lights
Diwali, the Festival of Lights, celebrates good over evil with lamps, prayers, fireworks, sweets, and family gatherings.
vijaydashmi
good over evil
Vijaydashmi celebrates Lord Rama's victory over Ravana, symbolizing good's triumph over evil with processions, effigies burning, and prayers.
Durga Puja
fight the evil
Durga Puja celebrates Goddess Durga's victory over Mahishasura, with grand decorations, rituals, prayers, cultural events, and feasting.
Holi
spread colors
Holi, the Festival of Colors, celebrates spring's arrival with vibrant powders, music, joy, and the triumph of good.
Raksha
Bandhan
Raksha Bandhan celebrates the bond between brothers and sisters, marked by tying a protective thread and exchanging gifts and blessings.
Janmashtami
birth of God
Janmashtami celebrates Lord Krishna's birth with prayers, fasting, singing, and reenactments of his life, symbolizing devotion and love.
Ganesh
Chaturthi
Ganesh Chaturthi honors Lord Ganesha's birth with prayers, clay idol installations, processions, and immersions, seeking wisdom and prosperity.
maha
Shivaratri
Maha Shivratri honors Lord Shiva with fasting, night vigils, prayers, and meditation, symbolizing overcoming darkness and ignorance.
Makar
sakranti
Makar Sankranti marks the transition of the sun into Capricorn, celebrating harvests with kite flying, sweets, and family gatherings.
